================================================================================
PART 1 — FOUNDATIONAL CONCEPTS
================================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------------------
1.1  SMILES  (Simplified Molecular Input Line Entry System)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
A SMILES string is a way of writing a chemical molecule as a line of text.
Instead of drawing a 2D structure on paper, you encode all the atoms and bonds
in a compact string format.

Example:
  Aspirin = CC(=O)Oc1ccccc1C(=O)O

Rules:
  - Atoms written as element symbols (C, N, O, S, F, Cl, Br, etc.)
  - Bonds: single (default), = double, # triple
  - Ring closures numbered: c1ccccc1 means a benzene ring
  - Branches in parentheses

Canonical SMILES: a standardized, unique form of SMILES so that the same
molecule always produces the same string, regardless of how you drew it.
This project uses "canonical_smiles" as the unique identifier for each molecule.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
1.2  QSAR  (Quantitative Structure-Activity Relationship)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
QSAR is the science of using the structure of a molecule to predict how active
or potent it will be in a biological test (assay).

The logic is:
  "Similar molecules tend to have similar biological activity."

What a QSAR model does:
  1. Takes a molecule's structure (SMILES)
  2. Converts it to a vector of numbers called molecular descriptors
  3. Runs those numbers through a trained machine learning model
  4. Outputs a predicted activity value (e.g., IC50 in micromolar)

Why it matters:
  Without QSAR, you must physically synthesize and test every new molecule
  in a lab, which takes weeks and costs thousands of dollars per compound.
  QSAR lets you pre-screen millions of virtual molecules in minutes.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
1.3  IC50  (Half-Maximal Inhibitory Concentration)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
IC50 is the most common drug potency measurement.
It represents the concentration of a drug needed to inhibit a biological
target by 50% in a test tube experiment.

Units: micromolar (uM) or nanomolar (nM)
  - Lower IC50 = more potent (less drug needed)
  - Higher IC50 = less potent (more drug needed)

Example: IC50 = 1 uM means 1 millionth of a mole per litre achieves 50% inhibition.

Conversions:
  pIC50 = -log10(IC50 in molar)
  Higher pIC50 = better (it is a log scale flipping the direction)
  pIC50 = 6 means IC50 = 1 uM
  pIC50 = 9 means IC50 = 1 nM (very potent)

Project threshold: IC50 <= 5 uM (pIC50 >= 5.3) is the minimum potency cutoff.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
1.4  ADME Properties
------------------------------------------------------------------------
ADME stands for:
  A = Absorption  (how well does the drug get into the bloodstream?)
  D = Distribution (does it reach the right tissues?)
  M = Metabolism   (how fast does the body break it down?)
  E = Excretion    (how does the body remove it?)

For oral drugs to work, they must pass ADME tests. This project predicts
ADME properties using computational models rather than experiments.

Key ADME properties used here:

  cLogP  (calculated LogP, lipophilicity)
    - LogP = log of the ratio of drug in oil vs water
    - Measures how "fat-soluble" vs "water-soluble" a molecule is
    - Too low (<0) = too water-soluble, may not cross cell membranes
    - Too high (>5) = too fat-soluble, may accumulate in fat, be toxic
    - Optimal range for this project: 1.0 to 3.5

  logS  (aqueous solubility, ESOL model)
    - Measures how well the drug dissolves in water
    - Units: log(mol/L)
    - More negative = less soluble
    - Poorly soluble drugs cannot be absorbed well
    - Threshold: logS >= -6.0 (anything more negative is too insoluble)

  Microsomal Clearance  (pred_MetStab_Clearance_Microsome_AZ)
    - Measures how fast liver microsomes (enzymes in test tubes) break down
      the drug
    - Units: uL/min/mg protein
    - Higher clearance = drug breaks down faster = shorter duration of action
    - Threshold: <= 70 uL/min/mg (any higher means the drug disappears too fast)

  Hepatocyte Clearance  (pred_MetStab_Clearance_Hepatocyte_AZ)
    - Same concept as microsomal but using whole liver cells (hepatocytes),
      which is closer to what happens in the body
    - Units: uL/min/10^6 cells
    - Threshold: <= 80 uL/min/10^6 cells

  Half-Life  (pred_MetStab_Half_Life_Obach)
    - The time for 50% of the drug to leave the body
    - Predicted using the Obach regression model
    - Units: hours
    - Longer half-life = less frequent dosing needed, better patient compliance
    - Threshold: >= 40 hours (once-daily dosing viability)

  Lipinski's Rule of Five  (Ro5)
    - A classic quick filter for oral drug-likeness:
      MW <= 500 daltons
      cLogP <= 5
      H-bond donors <= 5
      H-bond acceptors <= 10
    - Molecules passing all four are likely orally bioavailable
    - Named "rule of five" because all cutoffs are multiples of 5


------------------------------------------------------------------------
1.5  Molecular Descriptors
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Molecular descriptors are numbers calculated from a molecule's structure that
capture different chemical features:

  - Morgan Fingerprints: circular fingerprints encoding local neighborhoods
    around each atom; represented as bit vectors (0/1 for each possible
    circular substructure)
  - RDKit Descriptors: ~200 physicochemical properties (MW, logP, polar
    surface area, rotatable bonds, ring counts, etc.)
  - ECFP (Extended Connectivity Fingerprint): similar to Morgan, commonly used
    in QSAR

These descriptors are fed as input features to the machine learning model.


========================================================================
PART 2 — MACHINE LEARNING MODELS USED
========================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.1  CatBoost
------------------------------------------------------------------------
CatBoost is a gradient-boosting machine learning algorithm made by Yandex.
It is an ensemble of decision trees built sequentially, where each new tree
corrects the errors of all previous trees.

Why it is good for QSAR:
  - Handles mixed numeric/categorical data well
  - Robust to overfitting on small datasets
  - Built-in handling of missing values
  - Often outperforms random forest and standard gradient boosting on
    small chemistry datasets

In this project: CatBoost is the primary regression model for predicting
pIC50, cLogP, logS, microsomal clearance, hepatocyte clearance, and half-life.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.2  Random Forest
------------------------------------------------------------------------
An ensemble of many independent decision trees where each tree is trained
on a random subset of the data and features. Final prediction is the average
of all trees.

Advantages: very robust, interpretable uncertainty (standard deviation of
tree predictions can estimate confidence).


------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.3  Low-Data QSAR (Problem-Specific Approach)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Standard QSAR assumes you have hundreds or thousands of data points.
Low-data QSAR deals with situations where you have fewer than 100
experimental measurements.

Techniques used:
  - Data augmentation (SMILES enumeration: same molecule, different valid SMILES)
  - Transfer learning from larger public datasets (ChEMBL, PubChem bioassays)
  - Scaffold-stratified cross-validation to avoid data leakage
  - Conformal prediction for uncertainty quantification


========================================================================
PART 3 — REINVENT: THE MOLECULE GENERATION ENGINE
========================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.1  What is REINVENT?
------------------------------------------------------------------------
REINVENT is an open-source molecular generation library developed by
AstraZeneca. It uses deep learning (recurrent neural networks and transformers)
to generate new drug-like molecules.

Core idea: A language model is trained on millions of known drug-like SMILES.
After training it can generate new, valid SMILES it hasn't seen before — like
a language model generating grammatically correct new sentences.

Four generation modes are used in this project:

  REINVENT (plain)
    The base generative model creating molecules from scratch, sampling
    from the learned SMILES distribution.

  Mol2Mol
    Molecule-to-molecule model. You provide a seed molecule and it generates
    analogues — new molecules that are structurally similar to the seed.
    Good for lead optimization (take an active compound, explore nearby
    chemical space).

  PepINVENT
    A variant specialized for peptidic / macrocyclic molecules.
    Generates SMILES with peptide-like backbones.

  LibINVENT
    Scaffold decoration model. You provide a scaffold (fixed core ring system)
    and it decorates it with different substituents (R-groups) at defined
    attachment points.

  LinkINVENT / LinkINVENT-Transformer
    Fragment linker model. Given two molecular fragments, it designs a linker
    that connects them into a full drug candidate.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.2  Training Modes
------------------------------------------------------------------------

  Plain (Prior Sampling)
    No optimization — just sample new molecules from the pre-trained
    distribution. Good for exploring broad chemical space.

  RL  (Reinforcement Learning)
    The model is rewarded for generating molecules with desired properties
    (e.g., high predicted pIC50, good ADME) and penalized for undesirable
    properties. Over many steps it learns to generate better compounds.
    Analogous to training a dog with treats.

  CL  (Curriculum Learning)
    A training strategy where the model is first trained on easier, more
    drug-like samples and gradually exposed to harder optimization targets.
    Like teaching with progressively harder exercises — improves convergence
    and produces more valid SMILES.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.3  Prior vs. Agent
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Prior: the original pre-trained REINVENT model (trained on millions of
drug-like molecules from ChEMBL). It defines the "grammar" of valid SMILES.

Agent: a fine-tuned version of the prior, optimized toward a specific
scoring objective (your QSAR model outputs). The agent is what generates
the candidate molecules during RL/CL training.

The Prior acts as a regularizer — the agent is penalized for drifting too
far from the prior to prevent it from generating nonsensical SMILES while
still exploring new chemistry.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.4  TOML Configuration Files
------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOML (Tom's Obvious Minimal Language) is the configuration file format
used by REINVENT. Each run is specified in a .toml file that defines:
  - Which model to use (prior path, agent path)
  - Number of training steps (epochs)
  - Batch size (molecules generated per step)
  - Scoring function components (which QSAR predictions to optimize)
  - Diversity filters (to prevent mode collapse)
  - Output directories and log locations


------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.5  Diversity Filters
------------------------------------------------------------------------
During RL training, the model tends to collapse — generating the same
high-scoring molecule over and over instead of exploring new structures.
Diversity filters penalize repeated molecules or molecules from already-seen
scaffolds, forcing the model to keep exploring.

Types:
  IdenticalMurckoScaffold: penalize molecules sharing the same Murcko scaffold
  IdenticalTopologicalScaffold: stricter version
  ScaffoldSimilarity: gradual penalty based on scaffold similarity


========================================================================
PART 4 — SCORING AND ADME FILTERING
========================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------------------
4.1  Score Generated SMILES (score_generated_smiles_adme.py)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
After REINVENT generates a batch of SMILES, this script runs them through
the production scoring stack in this order:

  Step 1: SMILES validation and canonicalization
    RDKit parses each SMILES and canonicalizes valid molecules.
    Invalid molecules are removed immediately.

  Step 2: Descriptor calculation
    RDKit/ECFP-style descriptors are computed for each valid molecule.

  Step 3: QSAR potency prediction with uncertainty
    The best IC50 model produces the primary prediction (pred_pIC50,
    pred_ic50_uM).
    Up to five positive-R² models are also evaluated to estimate
    prediction disagreement (uncertainty columns).

  Step 4: ADMET and developability enrichment
    ADMET-AI endpoints and chemistry developability signals are added
    (for example toxicity risk proxies, DDI/CYP signals, absorption support,
    and synthetic accessibility where needed downstream).

  Step 5: Pass/fail and ranking-ready fields
    Molecules are labeled with pass flags and normalized fields required
    by Gate 1 ranking logic and downstream analysis.

  Step 6: Human-readable outputs
    CSV and Excel outputs are written, including embedded 2D structures
    to support medicinal-chemistry review.

  Output: *_generated_scored.csv and *_generated_scored.xlsx


------------------------------------------------------------------------
4.2  ADME Cutoffs Applied
------------------------------------------------------------------------
These are the precise thresholds a molecule must pass to be labeled ADME-clean:

  pred_ic50_uM           <= 5.0   (potent inhibitor)
  pred_cLogP             1.0–3.5  (balanced lipophilicity window)
  pred_logS_ESOL         >= -6.0  (adequately soluble)
  pred_MetStab_Clearance_Microsome_AZ  <= 70.0   uL/min/mg
  pred_MetStab_Clearance_Hepatocyte_AZ <= 80.0   uL/min/10^6 cells
  pred_MetStab_Half_Life_Obach         >= 40.0   hours


  ------------------------------------------------------------------------
  4.3  Why This Scoring Order (Rationale)
  ------------------------------------------------------------------------
  The sequence above is intentional and cost-aware:

    1) Validity first
      Do not spend compute on molecules that are chemically invalid.

    2) Potency and ADME second
      These are the strongest early indicators of whether a molecule is worth
      considering at all.

    3) Uncertainty before prioritization
      A high predicted potency with high model disagreement is risky.
      Uncertainty-aware ranking reduces false confidence and improves
      shortlist reliability.

    4) Translational risk later
      Tox/DDI/absorption are critical, but they are used after core potency
      and developability filtering to avoid over-penalizing broad exploration.

    5) Human-readable exports at every major handoff
      CSV supports automation; Excel with structures supports scientist review.


========================================================================
PART 5 — SCAFFOLD ANALYSIS
========================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.1  Murcko Scaffold
------------------------------------------------------------------------
A Murcko scaffold is the core ring system of a molecule with all chains
stripped off. It captures the structural "skeleton" that defines a compound
series.

Example:
  Full molecule: ethyl 4-aminobenzoate (procaine precursor)
  Murcko scaffold: just the benzene ring

Why it matters:
  Molecules from the same scaffold behave similarly in terms of binding pose
  and selectivity. By grouping molecules by their Murcko scaffold, you can:
    - Measure chemical diversity (how many different scaffolds were explored?)
    - Avoid submitting near-identical analogues for synthesis (saves cost)
    - Identify which scaffold series are most promising

  scaffold_bucket column: the canonical SMILES of the Murcko scaffold, or
  "acyclic_or_none" for molecules with no rings.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.2  Scaffold Diversity
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Scaffold diversity = the number of unique Murcko scaffolds in a set.

High diversity means the model explored many different chemical series.
Low diversity means the model converged on one scaffold family.

In the shortlist, the max-per-scaffold cap limits how many molecules from
the same scaffold can be selected, enforcing breadth of chemical space coverage.


========================================================================
PART 6 — THE HYBRID STAGE-GATED PIPELINE
========================================================================

The Hybrid Stage-Gated Pipeline is the main decision framework implemented
in reinvent_integration/run_hybrid_stage_gates.py.

The core idea: start with cheap fast computational filters and only escalate
the most promising candidates to expensive experimental or physics-based
methods. This mirrors how real drug discovery programs operate.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.1  Triage Score
------------------------------------------------------------------------
The triage score is a multi-objective composite score that converts all
individual ADME and potency predictions into a single 0–1 number for ranking.

Formula:
  triage_score = (
      0.35 × normalized_potency        +   (most important: IC50)
      0.15 × normalized_logS           +   (solubility)
      0.15 × clogp_window              +   (lipophilicity in sweet spot)
      0.10 × normalized_microsome      +   (metabolic stability, microsomes)
      0.10 × normalized_hepatocyte     +   (metabolic stability, hepatocytes)
      0.15 × normalized_half_life           (duration of action)
  )

Each component is min-max normalized to 0–1 before combining, so no single
property dominates unfairly.

cLogP window score: 1.0 if cLogP is between 1 and 3.5 (ideal), decays to 0
  as cLogP moves away from this window.

Higher triage score = better overall candidate.

Weight rationale (why these coefficients):
  - 0.35 potency: strongest objective because activity is non-negotiable.
  - 0.30 absorption-related balance (logS + cLogP window): compounds must be
    soluble and permeability-compatible to be useful in vivo.
  - 0.20 metabolic stability (microsome + hepatocyte): avoids fast-clearance
    liabilities that often fail in progression.
  - 0.15 half-life: rewards exposure duration, but not enough to overpower
    potency and developability.

Design principle:
  The score is not meant to be a mechanistic truth; it is a triage utility
  function to rank many candidates consistently before deeper filtering.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.2  Normalization
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Min-max normalization rescales a property to 0–1:
  normalized = (value - min_value) / (max_value - min_value)

"Higher is better" (pIC50, logS, half-life): normalized directly.
"Lower is better" (IC50, clearance): inverted so normalized value is high
  when raw value is low.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.3  Gate 1 — Computational Triage (REINVENT + QSAR)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
What happens:
  1. All REINVENT-generated molecules from all families and modes are pooled
     into a single master table.
  2. Each molecule is scored with the triage formula.
  3. Molecules are ranked highest-to-lowest by triage score.
  4. A scaffold-diverse shortlist is selected (default 200 compounds,
     maximum 3 per Murcko scaffold).

Output files:
  gate1_master_ranked.csv    — all candidates with ranks and triage scores
  gate1_shortlist.csv        — the top 200 diverse candidates
  gate1_shortlist.xlsx       — Excel with 2D images + analysis
  gate1_summary.json         — statistics (total candidates, unique scaffolds,
                               ADME pass rate)
  gate1_README.txt           — human-readable summary

Purpose: Rapidly select the most promising and diverse subset for deeper
analysis, discarding obvious weak candidates.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.4  Legacy Gate 2 / Gate 3 Note
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Gate 2 and Gate 3 have been removed from the active workflow.
Gate 1 is now the only active hybrid-stage shortlist stage in
reinvent_integration/run_hybrid_stage_gates.py.

Historical references to Gate 2 and Gate 3 in older reports are retained
only for context and are not produced by the current pipeline.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.6  Scaffold-Diverse Shortlist Selection
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Algorithm (select_diverse_shortlist function):
  1. Sort all candidates descending by triage score.
  2. Iterate through the sorted list.
  3. Track how many molecules from each scaffold have been selected.
  4. Accept a molecule if it:
       a. Has not reached the max-per-scaffold cap, AND
       b. The shortlist total has not reached top_k
  5. Reject the molecule if its scaffold is already at the cap.
  6. Continue until top_k molecules are collected or list is exhausted.

Effect: guarantees that no single scaffold family dominates the shortlist,
ensuring breadth in chemical space coverage.

PART 7 — EXCEL OUTPUT FORMAT
========================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------------------
7.1  Sheet 1: Compounds
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Each row is one molecule. Columns:
  rank                      Rank by triage score (1 = best)
  Structure                 2D drawing of the molecule (embedded PNG image)
  canonical_smiles          The unique SMILES string for the molecule
  triage_score              Composite score (0–1, higher is better)
  pred_pIC50                Predicted potency in pIC50 units
  pred_ic50_uM              Predicted IC50 in micromolar (lower is better)
  pred_cLogP                Predicted lipophilicity
  pred_logS_ESOL            Predicted aqueous solubility
  pred_MetStab_Clearance_Microsome_AZ    Microsomal clearance
  pred_MetStab_Clearance_Hepatocyte_AZ   Hepatocyte clearance
  pred_MetStab_Half_Life_Obach           Plasma half-life
  mol_weight                Molecular weight (daltons)
  passes_adme               1 = passes all 6 ADME cutoffs; 0 = fails one+
  source_family             Which REINVENT family generated this molecule
                              (reinvent, mol2mol, pepinvent, libinvent,
                               linkinvent, linkinvent_transformer)
  source_mode               Which training mode (plain, reinforcement_learning,
                               curriculum_learning)
  scaffold_bucket           Murcko scaffold of the molecule

Color coding:
  Green cells: good values (high pIC50, long half-life, passes ADME)
  Red cells:   bad values (high IC50, high clearance)


------------------------------------------------------------------------
7.2  Sheet 2: Analysis
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Five sections of summary statistics:

  Section 1 — Overall Summary Statistics
    Total candidates in shortlist
    ADME pass count and percentage
    Mean and standard deviation of triage score
    Unique Murcko scaffold count
    Mean molecular weight

  Section 2 — Breakdown by Source Family
    Table: for each REINVENT family (reinvent, mol2mol, pepinvent, etc.)
      Number of compounds
      Percentage of shortlist
      Mean triage score

  Section 3 — Breakdown by Training Mode
    Same table but split by plain / RL / CL

  Section 4 — Top 15 Scaffold Clusters
    The 15 Murcko scaffolds most represented in the shortlist:
      Scaffold SMILES
      Compound count
      Mean triage score for that cluster

  Section 5 — ADME Property Percentiles
    For each of the 7 ADME properties:
      10th percentile, 25th, 50th (median), 75th, 90th
    Lets scientists quickly see the distribution and whether the shortlist
    is collectively meeting targets.


========================================================================
PART 8 — FULL PIPELINE WORKFLOW (END TO END)
========================================================================

Step 1: QSAR Model Training (scripts/main.py / scripts/run_pipeline.py)
  Input:  experimental assay data (IC50 measurements + structures)
  Output: trained models and metadata used to build artifact bundles.

Step 2: Export QSAR Artifact (reinvent_integration/export_qsar_model.py)
  Packages the trained model + descriptor configuration into a single
  artifact file that scoring scripts can load.

Step 3: Configure REINVENT runs (configs/)
  Create TOML config files for each family × mode combination.
  Six families × three modes = up to 18 generation campaigns.

Step 4: Run REINVENT Generation (reinvent_integration/run_all_generation.py)
  For each config:
    a. Run REINVENT prior sampling or RL/CL training
    b. Sample final molecules at end of training
    c. Save sampled SMILES to results/{family}_{mode}/samples.csv

Step 5: Score All Generated Molecules (score_generated_smiles_adme.py)
  For each samples.csv:
    a. Validate SMILES with RDKit
    b. Compute descriptors
    c. Predict all 7 ADME/potency properties with QSAR models
    d. Label each molecule as ADME pass/fail
    e. Embed 2D structure images
    f. Save to outputs/generated/{family}/{mode}/*_generated_scored.csv

Step 6: Hybrid Stage Gates (run_hybrid_stage_gates.py)
  Gate 1: Pool all scored CSVs → compute triage score →
          rank → scaffold-diverse top-200 shortlist → Excel output

Step 7: Human Review
  Scientists open the Gate 1 Excel, visually inspect structures, check
  the Analysis sheet for distribution summaries, and select molecules
  for wet-lab follow-up.

Step 8: Wet Lab Synthesis and Testing
  Remaining molecules are synthesized and tested experimentally.
  Results feed back into QSAR model retraining (active learning cycle).

Overall rationale for the current design:
  Gate 1 maximizes breadth and speed while preserving scaffold diversity.
This shortlist-first structure supports fast iteration and clear handoff
to medicinal chemistry review.


========================================================================
PART 9 — KEY FILES AND WHAT THEY DO
========================================================================

  scripts/main.py
    Trains the QSAR model from scratch. Handles cross-validation, feature
    selection, model comparison (CatBoost vs Random Forest vs others).
    Used for full, explicit training workflows.

  qsar_core/model.py
    Defines the machine learning model classes, hyperparameter grids,
    training loops, and evaluation metrics (R2, RMSE, MAE).

  qsar_core/descriptors.py
    Converts a SMILES string into a vector of RDKit molecular descriptors.
    Also generates Morgan fingerprints for QSAR input.

  qsar_core/data_loader.py
    Reads experimental data from CSV/Excel, standardizes SMILES with RDKit,
    handles duplicates, splits data into train/validation/test sets.

  qsar_core/clustering.py
    Performs scaffold-based clustering to ensure molecules in train and test
    sets come from different scaffolds (avoids optimistic evaluation).

  qsar_core/visualization.py
    Generates plots: actual vs predicted scatter plots, residuals, feature
    importance, ADME distribution histograms.

  scripts/run_pipeline.py
    High-level orchestrator for the QSAR pipeline. Also contains
    generation preset dispatcher (fast/balanced/diverse modes).

  reinvent_integration/run_hybrid_stage_gates.py
    The core hybrid pipeline script. Discovers scored CSVs, computes triage
    scores, selects scaffold-diverse shortlist, writes gate artifacts.

  reinvent_integration/run_all_generation.py
    Orchestrates all REINVENT generation + scoring campaigns. Calls the
    hybrid stage gate script at the end.

  reinvent_integration/score_generated_smiles_adme.py
    Scores a batch of SMILES with QSAR models and writes Excel output.

  reinvent_integration/export_qsar_model.py
    Packages trained model for use by the scoring script.

  reinvent_integration/build_qsar_artifact.py
    Builds the artifact bundle: model + descriptor scaler + config.

  reinvent_integration/post_screen.py
    Post-processing filter applied after scoring to apply additional
    physicochemical rules before shortlisting.

  reinvent_integration/scaffold_all_configs.py
    Auto-generates all TOML config files for each family × mode combination.

PART 11 — COMPARISON: THIS APPROACH vs SIMULATION PLUS
========================================================================

This Project (REINVENT + QSAR + Hybrid Gates)
  Pros:
    Full generative chemistry: creates entirely new molecules, not just
      property predictions on existing compounds.
    Scaffold diversity built in via Murcko-diversity filter.
    RL/CL optimization actively drives molecules toward ADME targets.
    Modular: new ADME endpoints can be added without retraining REINVENT.
    Open source (REINVENT, RDKit, CatBoost) — no per-query cost.
    Explainable QSAR with feature importances.
    Now supports uncertainty-aware ranking using up to five positive-R² models.
  Cons:
    Requires training data for each ADME endpoint.
    Still depends on ligand-based evidence rather than target-resolved physics.
    Uncertainty is estimated from model disagreement, not from a real protein-ligand simulation.

Simulation Plus (GastroPlus / ADMET Predictor)
  Pros:
    Industry-standard PBPK platform; accepted by regulatory agencies (FDA).
    Models include gut absorption, first-pass metabolism, tissue distribution.
    Validated against large clinical datasets.
    Provides human PK predictions with confidence intervals.
  Cons:
    Does not generate new molecules — only predicts properties of molecules
      you already have.
    Black-box machine learning in newer models (limited interpretability).
    Expensive commercial license.
    Cannot optimize toward multiple objectives simultaneously.

Combined Strategy (current no-target production architecture):
  Use REINVENT + QSAR for broad generative exploration (Gate 1).
  Use Gate 1 shortlist exports for medicinal chemistry review and wet-lab planning.
  Preserve structure-based and PBPK folders only as legacy references when a
  real target or external platform becomes available in the future.

========================================================================

ADMET     ADME + Toxicity
AZ        AstraZeneca (the company whose clearance assay model is used)
CL        Curriculum Learning

ESOL      Estimated SOLubility (a specific logS prediction model)

GBM       Gradient Boosting Machine
